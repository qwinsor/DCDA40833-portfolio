Reflection & Analysis

Data Preparation

This dataset was extremely well organized. This allowed me to have an almost seamless experience while importing it into Tableau. The only thing that I had to do was change the category of the efficiency_score column to number.

Design Choices

the design was the hardest part for me as its hard to get creative with the way I display data. I chose to go with a straight forward bar chart for the majority of my charts, however the price vs. performance chart needed to be a scatterplot due to the number of variables I was displaying. My Price (fuel type vs. body type) chart was the most fun for me to make. I wanted to make a heatmap of the 6 countries that I have data on, however that didnt work out so i just made a 4 by 4 array instead. The final touch was using green because... money.

Expected Patterns

Most of the outcomes that I had drawn up in my head ended up being completely wrong. I assumed that the amount of horsepower a car had directly influenced the price of the car, however I failed to think about the different types of vehicles, and the fact that some of the most expensive cars arent necessarily that powerful.

The other thing I completely missed on was that the manufacturing year has nothing to do with the price either. I assumed that because this dataset is from 2005 to 2025 none of the cars were old enough to be coveted. This would mean that the newer cars are more expensive right? Wrong. For some reason the price seems to fluctuate with a peak and a floor every two years.

Surprises and Outliers

I think it is surprising that China has the most efficient cars on average. I always thought of China as a country that doesnt care about efficiency as long as the product is affordable.

Questions for Further Investigation

Maybe if I broke down the data more I could find out if the type of car or fuel has an effect on the efficiency of the car. I am sure that there is many ways to break find some sort of correlation other than the manufacturing country, it will just take more research.
